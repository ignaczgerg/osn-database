What is Organic Solvent Nanofiltration?

Organic Solvent Nanofiltration (OSN) or Solvent-Resistant Nanofiltration (SRNF) is a pressure-driven membrane separation technology that is capable of distinguishing and separating solutes based on their physicochemical parameters. OSN offers a low-energy and continuous tool in the pharmaceutical, food, and fine chemical industry.

OSN has been primarily used in petrochemical and pharmaceutical industries [1], solute recovery, solvent recycling, and purification. In addition, OSN is considered a green and potentially sustainable separation technology due to its low energy consumption and waste generation.

Why do we need a database?

Having a collection of scientific data would facilitate the development of membrane technology and their closely connected fields, such as chemistry and material science. Data-driven design would facilitate the traditionally slow trial-and-error material design methodologies.
