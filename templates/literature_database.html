Literature OSN Database

This dataset was constructed to establish predictive models for membrane performance that can fast-track and facilitate the industrial applications of organic solvent nanofiltration (OSN). As OSN performance is difficult to understand and predict due to its complex multi-dimensional relationship, we aim to perform data-driven prediction using a large set of well constructured experimental data.

The whole dataset consists of 42,290 datapoints with 4,229 rows and 10 columns compiled from 78 articles from 2002 onwards. Only commercial OSN membranes were considered. We included 10 parameters: the type of membranes, concentration of solute, MWCO of solute, testing solvent, transmembrane pressure, flux, permeance, rejection, operating temperature and process configuration.

In the published machine learning project, we first performed dimension reduction analysis using statistical PCA tool to better extract the underlying trends. Then, we applied three different algorithms: artificial neural network (ANN), support vector (SVM), and random forest (RF). The trained AI model showed up to 90% (RMSE: 1.133 for ANN and 0.905 for SVM; R2: 0.900 for ANN and 0.914 for SVM) and 91% (RMSE: 12.138 for ANN and 13.234 for SVM; R2: 0.914 for ANN and 0.906 for SVM) prediction accuracy for permeance and rejection, respectively.

The original article titled Artificial intelligence for performance prediction of organic solvent nanofiltration membranes was written by Jiahui Hu, Changsu Kim, Peter Halasz, Jeong F. Kim, Jiyong Kim and Gyorgy Szekely.
